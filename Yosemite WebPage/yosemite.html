Cool facts about yosemite

Image uploaded to pexels.com by' Andre Cook '

Yosemite valley is located in northen California and is part of Sierra Nevada mountain range. It is well known for it's steep granite cliffs that makes up the main valley and spectacular water falls that cascade into the valley. The geology of the Yosemite area is made up by granite rocks and remnants of older rock. About 10 million years ago, the Sierra Nevadas where beginning to form; an uplifted in the landscape increased the steepness of stream and rivers resulting in formation of deep canyons. About a million years ago, snow and ice formed, glaciers at the higher alpine meadows moved down the river valleys. The downslope movement of the ice masses sculpted the U-shaped valley that we see today.

Half Dome

Image uploaded to pexels.com by' Andre Cook '

Half Dome is a granite dome at the eastern end of Yosemite Valley in Yosemite National Park, California. It is possibly Yosemite's most familiar rock formation. The granite crest rises more than 4,737 ft (1,444 m) above the valley floor.

El Capitan

Image uploaded to pexels.com by' Andre Cook '

El Capitan is a vertical rock formation in Yosemite National Park, located on the north side of Yosemite Valley, near its western end. The granite monolith extends about 3,000 feet (900 m) from base to summit along its tallest face, and is one of the world's favorite challenges for rock climbers and BASE jumpers.

Glacier Point

Image uploaded to pexels.com by' Andre Cook '

Arguably the most spectacular view in all of yosemite national park. The point is located on the south wall of Yosemite Valley at an elevation of 7,214 feet and offers views of several well known landmarks including Yosemite Valley itself, Yosemite Falls, Half Dome, Vernal Fall, Nevada Fall, and Clouds Rest.
